Baking with Andrew: Classic Cinnamon Rolls

You will need:

  • 2 cups whole milk, warmed
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 stick of butter
  • 1 packet of active dry yeast (2 1/4 teaspoons)
  • 5 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons kosher salt
  • 3/4 cup light brown sugar
  • 12 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 tablespoons ground cinnamon
  • 1 can of vanilla frosting (optional)

Directions

  1. To make the dough whisk together the warm milk, sugar, and melted butter in a large bowl. The mixture should be slightly warm, not hot.
  2. Sprinkle the yeast evenly over the milk and butter mixture. Stir and allow it to rest for about 5 minutes.
  3. Add 4 cups of flour to the milk mixture and stir together until just combined.
  4. Cover the bowl with a kitchen towel or plastic wrap and rest it rise for 1 hour.
  5. Butter or grease 2 9-inch round baking pans and set aside.
  6. To make the filling mix together the brown sugar, butter, and cinnamon in a bowl.
  7. Remove the plastic wrap/towel from the dough and add the other cup of flour, baking powder, and salt. Stir well, then turn out onto a clean surface like a kitchen counter.
  8. Knead the dough for at least 10 minutes, adding more flour if necessary. Be careful not to add too much flour. The dough should be wet but should not stick to your hands or your surface. The dough should be smooth and spring back when poked once you've kneaded enough.
  9. Roll the dough out into a large rectangle, about 1/2 inch thick. Fix the corners with a spatula to make sure they're sharp and even.
  10. Spread the cinnamon filling out evenly over the dough.
  11. Starting from one end, roll up the dough into a log and pinch the seam closed. Place the seam side of the roll down facing your surface.
  12. Using a large kitchen knife, cut the log into evenly pieces, about 1 1/2 inches thick. Place the cinnamon rolls in the prepared pans, with 1 in the center and the other 5 surrounding it (almost like a star). Cover the pans with a towel and let proof for at least 30 minutes.
  13. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees Farhenheit.
  14. After the rolls are done proofing, bake them for 25-30 minutes until golden brown.
  15. Once the rolls are out of the oven, let them cool for at least 10 minutes before spreading any frosting/glaze over them.
  16. If wanted, add some store bought vanilla frosting over a warm roll. Enjoy!
